Before beginning step 3 of the migration process, you will need to meet with Editorial to decide on a 2- or 3-tiered navigation.

Structure & Layout

At this point, you should have all of your article pages organized in related content groups.

Now that you've met with Editorial, you have one of two wireframes in the document library on your team site.

It's up to you to fill the empty fields on the wireframe according to the higlighted (green sections). Notes have been provided in the "blank" wireframes to help inform your decisions.

Printing the wireframes and writing on them may be easiest for you. We're cool with this. Make sure to print in color and print a separate for sheet for each of the secondary pages. You can use the margins to make notes to editorial. Please write legibly.

You will work with Editorial to decide on one of the following layouts:

  1. CCSI Wireframe [Example from AAP.org]
    The CCSI layout is the best option for subsites that house a lot of content.
    • Page 1: Primary Landing Page. This is the new "home page" for your site. If your subsite has a logo, you'll need to get the original design files (.eps, .svg, .ai) to Ryan Watters.
      • The "multi-row widget" on the right side of the page can be filled with whatever content you'd like. It's okay to have duplication between the right rail and the content on the left if you feel that certain content needs to be promoted.
      • The left rail (navigation links on left of screen) titles are the titles of the secondary landing pages.
    • Page 2: Secondary Landing Page. Users land on these pages when selecting a left rail item. Further instruction is provided on the wireframe.
    • Page 3: Article Detail Page. Don't worry; we've already taken care of these.
  2. Secondary Page w Articles [Example from AAP.org]
    • Page 1. Landing Page. This is the home page for your site. Think about what you want featured on this page. Remember that the focus of your content should be in the center column of this page, since this is the first thing that shows up on mobile devices. Also keep a mobile device size in mind when deciding on the size of your site logo (i.e., if you have one)
    • Page 2. Article Detail Page. Don't worry; we've already taken care of these.

Note: The decision to include advertisements on every AAP.org web page was made at the executive level during the original refresh of AAP.org in 2011.

Design

It is easy to worry more about visual design than the structure and content of your site. This is why our process focuses on content first. The chosen layouts provide a nice, consistent user experience (UX) across multiple sites within the AAP.org site collection.

That said, if there are certain items you'd like to appear on the right rail of article or landing pages, be sure to make note of this as you are filling out your wireframes.

Once you have finished filling in your layout, please email the finished product to Ryan Watters. If you decided to print the wireframe, please drop it off at his desk.

Previewers

Before the site can be built, you will need to go to the "Site Preview" tab of your content audit worksheet and include the name and AAP ID number of anyone who will need to view the site before it goes live.

Editorial will begin building the site to your specifications and will contact you once a preview site is ready for review.
