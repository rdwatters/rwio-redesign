Auditing Existing Web Pages

Your content audit spreadsheet has a tab titled "Web Pages." This tab contains a list of all the web pages (ie., the Coldfusion .cfm/.html pages) from your current site.

To the right of each page you'll find the total pageviews for each page over the last 6 months. If the column is empty, analytics may have never been properly set up on your existing site.

The audit of your pages has two steps:

  1. Work through the entire list of pages and decide which pages you want to migrate (3rd column).
  2. After you've decided the pages to keep, go through each page and edit your content. You should read the basic best practices for web writing before you begin editing.

Linking Between Your New Pages

As you create your pages in Word, you may find yourself wanting to link to one of the new pages you're creating. You can do this by creating a hyperlink in MS Word and substituting ".aspx" for ".docx" in the filename.

If you want to create a link to the "events" pages within the body of your "about" page, you can do so my removing the abbreviation/naming convention and subsituting ".aspx" for ".docx.

The same would apply for the inverse use case. If you wanted to create a link to the "about" page in the body of the "events" page, you could write the link as a hyperlink in Word by appending ".aspx" to the name of the Word doc. As shown in the video—and continuing with the example above—you would simply enter "events.aspx" in the "Address" field of the Microsoft Word hyperlink popup.

Creating Logical Groups with Your Content

Once you create all of your new pages in Microsoft Word, you'll need to contact editorial so that we can start converting the pages into web pages for your new subsite.

While you wait for editorial to create the new pages, you can start putting your pages into related content groups. This will better inform your future decisions regarding layout and design. Ask yourself the following:

  • What kind of relationships do the pages have with one another?
  • How are my target end-users going to access this content?
  • What about my these new pages might confuse users?

And once you've finished making your groups:

  • Of these groups of content, which is the most important to showcase on the new site?

With few exceptions, you should have no more than 5 groups.

Now that you've completed your content audit, the most difficult part of your migration is over. Once the pages are finished being built in SharePoint, Editorial will meet with you to discuss layout options.
