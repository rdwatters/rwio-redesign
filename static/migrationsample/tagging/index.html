Tagging Content in AAP.org

All content housed in the AAP.org CMS must be tagged according to the new tagging structure.

Introduction
Tag Search

Introduction

It is extremely important that content owners tag all new pages and documents in the site collection.

There are 70+ metadata points (ie, groups of tags) available in AAP.org's CMS. To save time, Editorial has pared down the required metadata to the following fields:

  • Title. Title and filename are different. You can see the a filename when looking at a file on your desktop (e.g "mydocument.pdf"). A document title is at the top of an open page or document. Titles are the first piece of information to show up in search. Even images need a title. Files should be named according to the new naming conventions.
  • Subtitle. Subtitles are option for web pages and unnecessary for PDFs. However, you may want to consider a subtitle when adding metadata directly to your PDFs with Acrobat or Microsoft Word.
  • Topic. Topic is the overarching content theme. "Pediatrics Review and Education Program" is not a topic, per se, but could be an appropriate keyword.
  • Audience. Your audience is your ideal reader.
  • Managed Keyword. The current keyword site column in AAP.org already has approximately 550 managed keywords. Limit each article, web page, or document to 2 to 3 keywords in order of most to least relevant.
  • Location. Location is not a required field, but you should consider adding this tag to all of your content.

Tag Search

Tag Search was developed by Editorial to help you dynamically sift through the 1,0000+ managed tags/keywords on AAP.org. If you would like to suggest an additional tag for the AAP.org CMS, contact Ryan Watters.
