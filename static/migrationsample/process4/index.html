Review Your Site

Step 4 is the shortest and easiest step of your migration. At this stage, you should have already received an email from Editorial saying that the site is ready for review.

This is the last step before publication. Take your time to review all the content on your site. Make sure to involve other members of your team to provide feedback on any minor tweaks you would like for Editorial to make before the site is published live to AAP.org.

Final Tweaks

You will invariably find small things that you'd like to change on the site. Rather than request these changes piecemeal, it is better to use the "Site Preview" tab on your content audit spreadsheet. It is the responsibility of the content owner to gather all final requests for changes and list them under "Final Changes and Requests for the Site."

Once you are ready for the final changes to be made, select the email icon below:

Once the changes listed in the worksheet are complete, you'll be notified that your new site is now live. If there are any tweaks that could not be completed in your final list, you'll see comments under the "Editorial Comments" column to the right of the request.

Congratulations.

Future Changes

Now that your site is live, you can use the AAP.org request system (aap.org/websites) for future changes. All notifications in this system are automated.

Note: Submitting new content (e.g., new PDFs or new web pages) to your site requires the same front matter and naming conventions.
