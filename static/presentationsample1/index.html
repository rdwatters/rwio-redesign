Improving Discoverability

Life Preserver Content Analysis
Leading up to the meeting, I peformed a content analysis of the existing NIPC site section and Life Preserver, in particular.

What's the most commonly cited issue with Life Preserver?

People can't find it.

1 Year of Analytics (May 1, 2015 - May 1, 2016)

  • 1,211 pageviews (homepage)
  • 858 pageviews (≈ 15 pages)
  • 67% - 100% Bounce rates
The most common shortcoming of Life Preserver cited by the NIPC is that the program has a low level of discoverability within the larger cap.org website, and, as a result, the content within LP gets very little traffic. Google analytics show all pages that fall underneath the homepage for Life Preserver received just 858 pageviews between May 1, 2015 and May 1, 2016, which speaks to the infrequent traffic to the resource. That said, the bounce rates, defined by frequency with which a user leaves the entire website from that page, are higher than the site average. So the question becomes: how can people find Life Preserver?

So, how do we improve the discoverability of Life Preserver?

  1. Improve search engine optimization (SEO)
  2. Create engaging, original content
  3. Take a web-first approach
  4. Keep users on the site
So now that we know we need to shift our focus to improve discoverability, what incremental improvements can we make to Life Preserver?

SEO Improvement #1: Titling

Use language that is human and machine readable. New titling is considered an easy win.

  • Life Preserver vs pathology career development
  • Learn the Best Questions to Ask a Pathologist vs Questions Every New in Practice Pathologst Should Ask Senior Colleagues
  • Distinction between Make the Transition from Fellowship to Residency vs Adjust to Practice Life
While search algorithms are incredibly robust, we need to accept that at the end of the day, search crawlers ("spiders") can only parse text, and they are typically not smart enough to discern complex linguistic concepts (eg, metaphors). One easy way to improve SEO is by changing our title. The following are just examples that I'd like you to keep in mind when we move to the third part of this section (ie, changing the title of Life Preserver).

SEO Improvement #2: Establish credibility

Check! Content on CAP.org will already be considered "credible" by external search engines because of the organization's reputation and status as a nonprofit.

Google does not reveal all the benchmarks used to improve relevancy, but there are currently over 200. One well-established piece of SEO currency is developing credibility. Luckily, cap.org already exceeds this benchmark because of our ties to the organization's scholarly publishing branch, links from other reputable sites, our high level of traffic, 501c status, etc.

SEO Improvement #3: Create web-first content

Nielsen described PDFs as "unfit for human consumption"...

in 2003.

I cannot emphasize this enough. That said, "web-first" content does not require a high degree of technical proficiency. That said, we want to make sure the preferred, first-class citizen in terms of delivery medium is a webpage - this means fewer PDFs, Word documents, Excel spreadsheets, etc. While our current platform has limitations in this regard, we need to focus on a web- and content-first approach before we consider a potential replatform, rebranding, etc.

SEO Improvement #4: Keep Users on the Site

  • Create long-form content (≈1200 words or more).
    • Gone are the days of all web content needing to be short.
  • Drive users to content within CAP.org.
  • Do not send users to external sites.
  • Avoid lists that emulate a search engine results page (SERP)
Lastly, we should keep users on our site. We don't want them to bounce like they usually do...to other sites, to PDFs, to print documents, etc. Now it's just a matter of creating content that keeps them around...
